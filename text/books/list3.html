Pure Gobi
This book is inspired by John McPhee's description of Los Angeles rainfall in Control of Nature. This book employs burn imagery as tacit reminder of the aggressive use of natural resources in China. April 2003(38/35)

more, better
The Uncle Justin Series brings to you pint-size book with graphs packed with data. What happens when better is graphed onto more? The Law of Diminishing Returns is disproved inconclusively. The book has one pop-up and the graphs were created using Mathematica. May 2003. (40/40)

Mango
Can someone lust after a piece of fruit? Apparently so in June 2003's book. In an accordian fold book, a sinful lust of mangos is simply detailed in a dreamlike state. Imagery done on Adobe Illustrator. (40/40)

plants/overpass
Just imagine plants growing wild and reasserting their dominance upon the animal kingdom. Sounds farfetched? In August 2003, take a closer look at human roadways; the future is nigh. (40/40)
